Academic Interests

My primary academic interest lies at the intersection of biology and chemistry, with a specific focus on chemical reactions at the microscopic level. I am deeply fascinated by processes such as genetic inheritance and mutation, intracellular signaling, and intercellular communication—all of which are driven by underlying chemical mechanisms. Rather than relying heavily on interdisciplinary approaches like statistics or computational modeling, I prefer to engage directly with the biochemical foundations of life.

My long-term goal is to contribute to medically meaningful advances, such as developing cancer therapeutics by uncovering the chemical pathways that regulate cell division and differentiation. By deepening our understanding of how biomolecules interact, react, and sometimes malfunction in disease, I hope to help translate basic biochemical research into tangible benefits for human health.

Hospital Volunteer Service Research Project

Psychological Investigation and Strategy of Hospital Volunteer Service from the Public Perspective

Grade 10 Summer Project | School Competition Third Prize

Project Introduction

This project investigates the psychological motivations and service experiences of hospital volunteers from the public’s viewpoint, aiming to enhance the effectiveness and sustainability of volunteer programs in healthcare settings. Through a mixed-method approach—including surveys of 130 respondents and follow-up interviews with hospital staff and volunteers—the study identifies key challenges such as low long-term participation, time constraints among young and middle-aged volunteers, and the need for more engaging training and incentive mechanisms.

Findings reveal that while public willingness to volunteer is high, actual sustained involvement remains limited. The research also highlights differences in participation patterns across age groups and suggests that improved publicity, optimized scheduling, better training support, and thoughtful incentive structures could significantly boost volunteer retention and engagement.

Based on the analysis, the project proposes practical strategies—such as strengthening collaboration with schools and communities, enhancing pre-service training, and providing necessary protective measures—to build a more motivated, efficient, and sustainable hospital volunteer service system.
