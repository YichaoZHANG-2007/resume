Academic & Financial Plan

Academic Objectives

My primary academic ambition is to pursue advanced research in chemistry and biology, with a focused interest in functional genomics, physiological genetics, and the chemical reaction mechanisms at the cellular level. I am particularly driven to understand life's evolutionary trajectory from the perspective of genetic variation and to solve medical challenges, such as deciphering the mechanisms of cell division and differentiation to contribute to novel therapies for diseases like cancer.

I am convinced that a multidisciplinary approach is essential for modern life sciences, and I plan to integrate tools from statistics and computer science, including AI modeling, to decipher patterns in cellular processes and uncover fundamental principles of life.

Academic Journey

I have built a strong foundation for these goals through rigorous research training. Being selected for a national "Youth Talent Program" allowed me to engage in a project on "Quantitative Structure-Activity Relationship Modeling of Chemical Reactions." Through this experience, I progressed from applying standard linear regression to independently proposing data enhancement strategies and conducting comparative analyses of modeling techniques like SISSO.

This not only honed my practical skills in data analysis and cross-disciplinary methodology but also fostered a flexible, creative mindset, moving beyond seeking a single "best" method to developing scenario-specific strategies for complex problems.

I possess strong English communication skills and have studied Japanese to a daily conversational level, with the goal of taking the JLPT N2 exam. This ensures I can fully participate in English-taught programs and engage in academic life. My long-term plan is to proceed to master's and doctoral studies after my undergraduate degree, aiming for a research career where I can make meaningful contributions to the life sciences.

Academic Plan

To achieve my objectives, I have designed a structured four-year plan to fully utilize university resources:

First Year

My focus will be on building a solid academic foundation by excelling in core courses in biology. I will simultaneously continue to enhance my Japanese and English proficiency to ensure smooth communication. I intend to seek laboratory placements during winter and summer breaks from the first year to gain early hands-on research experience.

Second and Third Years

I will delve deeper into specialized biological theory and experimental techniques. To build an integrated interdisciplinary knowledge system, I will supplement my major with relevant courses in statistics and computer science. Active participation in seminars and workshops will help me identify specific research interests and refine my experimental design skills.

Fourth Year

I plan to join a research laboratory for a substantial internship or thesis project, allowing me to explore a specific direction in depth. This experience will serve as a capstone for my undergraduate studies and a bridge to future graduate research.

My ultimate goal is to contribute to the scientific community by unraveling the mysteries of life at a micro-level. I am committed to immersing myself fully in my studies and research, and I am confident that my focused interests and preparatory work make me a strong candidate for your program.
